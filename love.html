Grow Up And Blow Away

she flies into desperate longing
secretly, in the quiet after a meal
when the clock strikes and the house grows
larger and quicker into the collapse of night
splayed on the mattress, the weary lamps
from her childhood, like altar candles in the dark.
she goes to the place she always does:

the sea of office windows form the rising sun,
shapes and colors standing harsh and barren,
high purple accelerating on blocks of shadow,
the afternoon reprieve, where I hopped the fence
with dad, to find the rotting railway car
fields matted and melted, dead mice in the grass.

the ones who made it-
placing themselves in cracking pastel balconies
smoking in the morning, they are not found
waking and forgetting, vision fraying at the edges
looking out at the horizon and seeing only empty color
street lights fading forever into that great loneliness
they are cruel, they are serene.

And now I am there, and how rare and how excruciating
it feels to find it exactly as I hoped.





The Lot (Mannheim)

suddenly my path
is only stretching blocks of concrete
a space too big for using
afternoon sun careening off
sending warm red ripples my way
the edges too bounded
brushed in weeds and rotting cars


one lonely bicycle
is speeding towards the beating center
her dad is lingering
at the edge with me
we both sense the danger
of this too human expanse
holding us up in cracked hands
offering us into a bleeding sky


















working on colors

clocktower - coal black
the sky behind you cools to that murky blue
expanse - the color that never softens
as I get older
the color of the sky above the campfire
stars blinking cold as we drove over
the final stretch of gravel road
but there's something else on you - clocktower
a new color to remember
the way the setting sunlight clings to you
orange seeping down your side
deep purple bricks dripping
peach pie.
