BACK


the sky has darkened now as you are no longer in the city - the night sky because you are running and running in the pitch black night with only the sliver of yellow moon behind you.

its not pain that you feel or happiness or sadness or love or longing but really the discomfort of your body and the ground and the air - with perhaps excitement because it is night and you are alone. perhaps there are thoughts running too - you have looked up at the new bend in the trees and realized you are somewhere else. How did you go so far without remembering?

you are in the next town now, leaving the darkest stretch of road and the streetlights are here again, the sky is blue now, the houses are lit even up on the hills. empty streets so you can run in the center, but the trees by the sidewalk on the riverbank hold fireflies. you are so thirsty now and there is no water, you haven't even turned around so it will be ages until you have water, so you are desperate.

on the bank by the trees with the fireflies is a building, hard lines and unknown contents. on the side of the building a door is open, light seeping into the parking lot and it looks so flimsy, all of it. the wall of the building looks as small as cardboard, the white florescence around the door is dull, washed out against the streetlights. but you were looking for water, you are so thirsty.

You go to the door and inside is a grey room, the kind you expected - tile floors, halogen light overhead. you tread careful here. you go to the desk and rifle through the drawer, there is no sound but the passing of the papers, the hooks of your fingers, and here is where you start remembering -

I'm afraid my head has slipped away
slunk off in soft remembering.

I promise you I wishe I heard

notes breaking through the crashing waves

    Meeting new people 

    Entering your room
    posters scraped and taped to the walls
    light misting it all through pink curtains
    books stacked low, piled like treasure
    you tell me you make music here
    speak with your friends in the morning
    you show me your collection
    of the sounds of waves crashing.

    You tell me about the parties here
    when this room is filled, crushing
    a mass caught in the sound
    vibrating out into the dead night
    ringing heavy through the streets

    Your shirts are hung on the walls
    cold sweat soaked into the threads
    they carried your body moment to moment
    protecting you, collecting dirt in the fabric
    There's echos of those nights,
    when you caught the sounds of waves crashing,
    and gave them away.

    Will any of me be left here?
    soaked in your walls, to be felt by someone
    tripping, heaving, too far gone
    I'm sorry I went quiet,
    and left your perfect apartment.
    But what could I ever say
    in this room sopped in your memories  
    worth hearing?

            I've just swept
            let me show you the things I collect
            things I hold close, carry, extensions of me 
            things I fold myself into, neatly
            ticket stubs in my scrapbook, perfect

            the dust mites in my hair

            my avoidance of the mirror

            have fallen into your things too.   
            


    		





                        
   


    My feelings for you 

    When I used to write some things came easy
    writing about you, for instance.
    Imagining you felt good in my mouth
    like sour candy.
    Daydreaming tore my stomach,
    a headrush like holding your breath,
    or choosing not to eat.
    
    Writing about you felt important
    and it never seemed to run dry
    it spilled out, curdled and kept coming
    and I thought it would always come.
    until far under my gaze
    in the back of the cellar where I kept it
    the soft pink text fell apart
    became sticky in my fingers
    until there were no words at all
    and today I'm not even sure,
    which you you are anymore.
