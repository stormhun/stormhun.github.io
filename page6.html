A thoughtful reponse to
Joseph von Eichendorffs' Der Gefangene (1826)

On cobblestones and linden bones
bricks laden with fruiting vines
the vieled door again beckoning
for me to take my seat inside.

Golden words from time vorbei
fall in crowns atop my bowing head
I let the song of yesteryear revive
fertile valleys long since dead.

Oh sweet glimmering morning!
Oh nightly clouds that ev'r clear!
today bestoys Heidelbergs' sweet father -
Joseph von Eichendorff is here!

I kneel before the sodden lyrics
lead his Knights and Nixen to my core
let red mouths kiss my mild neck
and flowing Jungfraus paint my shore.

But halt schön Eichendorff
sweet nightengale you've ceased to sing!
I'm afraid my head has slipped away
slunk off in soft remembering.

I promise you I wishe I heard
what Professor Doctor Rösch had said
but playing mermaids could not dislodge
visions of the body laying in my bed.

Eichendorff calls out in urgence
notes breaking through the crashing waves
I'm deaf but for the lucid flower
of their breathing yesterday.

It took until the final line -
'her arms around him, soft and bare'
why title it 'the prisoner'?
when he is only caught in dreamy air?
