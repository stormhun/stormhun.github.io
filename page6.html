TRAPPED IN OUR MAZE :
ON RARE DAYS
(IMPOSSIBLY)
WE CONNECT

From writer's group, inspired by Emily Dickinson's 'Fame is a bee.'

Addiction is like a deer
she can move so lithely
before she startles
crashing through the brush
staring into the speeding car
with eyes that do not see.





(Also from group this week, what a productive session)

The Lot (Mannheim)

suddenly my path
is only stretching blocks of concrete
a space too big for using
afternoon sun careening off
sending warm red ripples my way
the edges too bounded
brushed in weeds and rotting cars


one lonely bicycle
is speeding towards the beating center
her dad is lingering
at the edge with me
we both sense the danger
of this too human expanse
holding us up in cracked hands
offering us into a bleeding sky
